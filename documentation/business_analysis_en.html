System Actors

Identification of key users who will interact with the address management system.

  • Kindergarten Administrator: Manages all information, users, roles.
  • Secretary: Enters, updates, and consults student, parent, and staff data.
  • Teacher: Consults contact information for students and parents assigned to their course.
  • Parent/Guardian (optional): Consults their own information and that of their children (with limited access).

Project Scope

Clear definition of what the system will include and what it will not.

Includes:

  • Management of student information (ID, first name, middle name (optional), paternal surname, maternal surname, preferred surname, nickname (optional), birth date, address, emergency contact, classroom, shift).
  • Management of parent/guardian information (ID, first name, middle name (optional), paternal surname, maternal surname, preferred surname, relationship, address, phone, email, authorized for student pickup (boolean), authorized for student changes (boolean)).
  • Management of staff information (ID, first name, middle name (optional), paternal surname, maternal surname, preferred surname, address, phone, email, classroom, role).
  • Search and filtering functionalities for information.
  • Generation of basic lists (e.g., student list by classroom, emergency contact list, staff attendance, student attendance).
  • Classroom management (students, attendance, activity teacher schedules (e.g., music, body expression, etc.)).
  • Attendance management in relation to the calendar: holidays, ordinary annual leave, sick leave, personal leave, etc. (students and staff).
  • Calendar management (holidays, leave, activities: annual, monthly, weekly, and daily; kindergarten, community, country, classroom).
  • Internal communication (messaging between users: parents with classroom staff of their children, and among all staff).

These details will form the basis for data modeling, which will be explored in the Data Modeling and Processes section.

Excludes:

  • Financial management (payments, invoicing).

Use Cases and Acceptance Criteria

Detailed description of actor interactions with the system and criteria for considering a functionality complete.

Examples:

For an extension and detail of use cases, consult the Data Modeling and Processes section.

  • Use Case: Register New Student.
  • Acceptance Criteria: The system must validate mandatory fields (first names, surnames, birth date). The system must associate the student with at least one parent/guardian. The system must save the student's primary address.

Current Processes and Problems

Documentation of current address management processes in the kindergarten and the problems the new system seeks to solve.

  • Problem: Contact information dispersed across different documents (paper, spreadsheets).
  • Problem: Difficulty quickly finding a student's address in case of emergency.
  • Problem: Manual and error-prone process for updating information.
